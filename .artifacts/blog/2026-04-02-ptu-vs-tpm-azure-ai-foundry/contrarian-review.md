# Contrarian Review

## Strongest critique

The post could be seen as anti-PTU, but PTU is legitimately cheaper for high-utilization workloads with stable patterns.

## Counter-arguments to include

- PTU *is* better if you have accurate forecasts and >60% utilization. The post acknowledges this.
- Some teams will be best served by PTU from day one (e.g., 24/7 customer-facing APIs with stable volume).

## Credibility risks

- Appearing to favor TPM without acknowledging PTU benefits.
- Oversimplifying the decision as data-driven when organizational factors (risk appetite, procurement cycles) also matter.

## Improvements applied

- Included explicit PTU benefits in breakdown.
- Framed as "data-driven decision" allowing org to pick based on their risk profile.
- Kept tone neutral on both options.
