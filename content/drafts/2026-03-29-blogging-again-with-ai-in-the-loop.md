I am back to writing regularly, and this time I am doing it with AI in the loop.

Not AI in charge.
AI in the loop.

That distinction matters more than the tooling.

Blogging has always been a growth engine for me.
I think of this blog as my own little book, written over time.

I have tried to blog for years, even before I moved to Norway.
I often got stuck in the same loop: no time to edit, fear of saying the wrong thing, and too many drafts that never shipped.

Now I am using ANI to break that loop.

## Why this matters

I want readers to feel motivated that they can do this too.

AI is not new, but the last few months have felt different.
The tools are finally good enough to help with the part that blocked me most: getting from idea to publishable draft.

ANI helps me build context.
I still do the real writing.

This blog is mine, not AI's.
ANI is my assistant.

## What changed

For me, the problem was never ideas.
It was execution.

I could create endless draft starts and still publish nothing.

ANI changed that by giving me momentum:

1. It asks me direct questions about the article.
2. It helps organize context and structure.
3. It pushes me from rough idea to clear draft.

Then I step in and do what only I can do:

1. Rewrite with my voice.
2. Remove anything that does not sound like me.
3. Add nuance from my own experience.
4. Proofread every section before publish.

ANI has issues, but I am still the driver.

## Framework or model

I am using a simple model for every post:


1. ANI interviews me with focused questions so my authentic self shows up.
2. ANI helps build a writing-style knowledge graph so the tone stays consistent.
3. I do final editorial control and own every published word.

My quality bar is simple:
If I cannot stand behind every paragraph in a live conversation, it does not ship.

AI helps with speed.
I own the judgment.

## Practical implementation

If you are trying to restart blogging, this is the workflow that helped me:

1. Pick one idea you care about.
2. Ask ANI for 10 to 15 targeted questions about that idea.
3. Answer in your own words, no filtering.
4. Turn those answers into a draft.
5. Edit until it sounds exactly like you.
6. Publish when you can defend every claim.

This workflow is practical for builders, people getting back into blogging, and first-time writers.

It keeps you moving without losing your voice.

## Risks and trade-offs

AI-assisted writing is not magic.

Risks are real:


The biggest one is simple: you can move faster and still end up sounding generic.

So I treat ANI as a partner, not an authority.
If it does not sound like me, it does not stay.

Also, criticism will happen.
That is fine.

There are 8 billion people on this planet.
Some will not like this approach.
Some will be inspired.

If I inspire one person to start writing again, that is a good deed.

## What to do this week

Set up an AI-assisted writing workflow for yourself:

1. Start one real post this week.
2. Use ANI to ask you targeted questions.
3. Answer honestly.
4. Edit for your voice.
5. Publish imperfect but real.

Never stop pushing.
Try and try again.

ANI is going to help me get my ideas out faster.
I hope they join me for the ride.

If this helps, follow along.
