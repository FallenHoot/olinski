Cloud CoEs were created to manage chaos when enterprises first adopted cloud.

Rules, tooling, guardrails, training.

Valid in 2020 when cloud was new.

By 2026, the landscape changed.

## Why this matters

CoEs work when:

- Teams are new to cloud (need guidance).
- Compliance requirements are tight (need central review).
- Cost sprawl is high (need central controls).

CoEs create friction when:

- Teams have cloud expertise (guidance becomes overhead).
- Compliance is mostly in code (can be automated).
- Cost controls are per-team (no central sprawl).

## Framework or model

Options:

1. **Full CoE:** Central team, review gates, training, compliance. Best for regulated industries, new enterprises.
2. **Platform only:** Central infrastructure platform; teams use self-service, minimal gates. Best for mature, large enterprises.
3. **Lightweight CoE:** Part-time owner, guidelines in docs, review only on exceptions. Best for smaller enterprises.

## What to do this week

1. Audit your current CoE effectiveness: Is it enabling or blocking?
2. Get honest feedback from teams on review cycle and guidance quality.
3. Decide: Do you need the current structure, or is it time to evolve?

CoEs are a transition tool.
You should move past them if you can.
