Every company is talking about building an AI Center of Excellence.

Most should not.

An AI CoE works when it owns:

1. Shared tooling (provisioning, APIs, model access).
2. Governance (approval gates, risk review).
3. Training (upskilling teams).

An AI CoE fails when it becomes:

1. A bottleneck on every project (approves all uses).
2. A separate team building solutions (becomes just another engineering team).
3. A label with no actual authority (people ignore it).

## Why this matters

AI governance is real and important.

But governance can be light-weight (shared platform, clear rules, fast lanes).

Or it can be centralized (everything goes through CoE).

The centralized model creates dependency and delays.

## Framework or model

Instead of a traditional CoE, use a platform model:

- **Core platform team:** Maintains shared AI infrastructure, model registry, cost controls.
- **Distributed domain teams:** Build solutions using platform, self-service.
- **Review gate (lightweight):** Compliance, security, cost; quick turnaround (2-3 days).

This avoids the CoE bottleneck while maintaining governance.

## What to do this week

1. If you don't have AI governance, build it (platform team + review gate).
2. If you have a CoE, audit whether it is enabling or blocking.
3. Get honest feedback from projects waiting for CoE approval.

Governance without bottlenecks.

That is the goal.
