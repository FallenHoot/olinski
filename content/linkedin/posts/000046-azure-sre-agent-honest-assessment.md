Last week I wrote about the four FinOps questions nobody wants to ask. This week: who answers them.

Most FinOps teams are one person spending 20% of their time. They can see every cost problem in the environment. They cannot fix any of them. They do not have the SRE skills, the monitoring access, or the permissions to change the infrastructure.

I was at a FinOps workshop at IBM Norway a few weeks ago. Someone showed a project that made me realize: the intelligence already exists. The data is already there. What is missing is an operations partner that never sleeps.

So I built one. Open-sourced it. 16,000 lines of PowerShell became 200 lines of YAML.

I wrote about what agentic AI changes for lean teams, what it changes for mature teams, and why FinOps without operations is just a dashboard.

What happens to the FinOps recommendations at your company? Do they become actions, or tickets?
