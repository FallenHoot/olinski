Cloud CoEs have established governance, tooling, and review workflows.

Adding AI to that existing structure is easier than building from scratch.

## Why this matters

Cloud CoEs own:

- FinOps and cost optimization.
- Compliance and security gates.
- Infrastructure standards.
- Team training.

AI governance fits naturally into this.

You don't build a separate AI CoE; you extend the existing one.

## Framework or model

Additions to existing Cloud CoE:

1. **AI Model Registry:** Catalog approved models, licensing, usage restrictions.
2. **Cost Controls:** AI-specific spending guardrails (token budgets, monthly caps).
3. **Compliance Gates:** Privacy, bias, data retention for AI systems.
4. **Training:** AI fundamentals, responsible AI, model evaluation.

## Practical implementation

1. Audit your existing Cloud CoE scope.
2. Layer AI governance into existing gates (do not create new ones).
3. Assign AI owner to CoE (could be 10-20% of existing role).
4. Add AI to monthly CoE steering committee.

## What to do this week

1. If you have a Cloud CoE, schedule meeting with CoE lead.
2. Propose adding AI governance to existing review gates.
3. Draft baseline AI policies (approved models, cost limits, compliance).

Leverage existing structure.
Avoid parallel governance.
