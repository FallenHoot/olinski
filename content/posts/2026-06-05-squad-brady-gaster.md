Squad is an open-source team coordination tool created by Brady Gaster.

It is not a replacement for Slack or Teams.

It is lightweight framework for coordinating distributed engineering teams.

Think: minimal ceremony, maximum async, team autonomy.

## Why this matters

Distributed teams need:

- Lightweight decision sharing (not Slack threads).
- Async communication (not Zoom calls).
- Clear ownership (who is responsible?).
- Low overhead (frameworks that get out of the way).

Squad tries to solve this.

## Framework or model

Squad is based on:

- Small, autonomous squads (5-8 people).
- Clear ownership (one owner per initiative).
- Async documentation (decisions written down, not meetings).
- Status updates posted (not pulled).

## Practical implementation

1. Check out https://github.com/bradygaster/squad.
2. Consider if your team is bigger than 50 (where you need a framework for async coordination).
3. If yes, try Squad's model for one sprint.

Squad is team operating system.

Minimal, direct, async.
