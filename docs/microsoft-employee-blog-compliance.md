# Microsoft Employee Blog Compliance

This policy applies to all blog drafting, review, and publishing steps in this repository.

## Source of truth

- Use only publicly available information.
- Prefer Microsoft Learn, official Azure documentation, and public blog posts.
- Do not rely on internal knowledge, assumptions, or non-public context.
- If information is not clearly public, exclude it.

## Absolute prohibitions

Never include or reference:

- Internal roadmaps, ETAs, or future plans.
- Capacity constraints, recovery timelines, or regional shortages not publicly documented.
- Internal emails, decks, tickets, chats, screenshots, or internal metrics.
- Customer names, architectures, incidents, or configurations unless already public and approved.
- Internal decision rationale or how Microsoft decided something.
- Speculation framed as insider knowledge.
- Anything that reads like a Microsoft announcement.

When in doubt, remove the content.

## Allowed content

- Technical explanations based on public documentation.
- Architecture patterns already published by Microsoft.
- Educational walkthroughs using GA features.
- Personal learnings framed generically without insider context.

Preview features must be labeled Preview with no promises.

## Language and framing rules

- Write as an individual, not as Microsoft.
- Do not imply official guidance, endorsement, or commitments.
- Avoid phrases such as:
  - Inside Microsoft
  - What we see internally
  - Microsoft will soon
- Avoid speculation.
- Use a neutral educational tone.

## Mandatory disclaimer

Include this statement (or equivalent) once in each publish-ready post:

"I work at Microsoft. The views expressed here are my own and based solely on publicly available information. This content is for educational purposes and does not represent official Microsoft guidance or commitments."

## Media and PR guardrails

- Do not write content intended for journalists.
- Do not frame posts as news or announcements.
- Do not quote executives or internal teams unless already public.

## Final compliance check

Before output, verify:

- All facts are publicly documented.
- No future-looking statements.
- No internal context or implied insider access.
- No customer or confidential references.
- Clear separation between opinion and fact.

If any check fails, revise or remove.

## Priority rule

When in doubt, omit. Compliance is more important than completeness.
